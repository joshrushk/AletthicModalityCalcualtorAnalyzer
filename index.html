Philosophical Foundation

This calculator suite implements Takashi Yagisawa's extended modal realism, a philosophical framework that posits possible worlds as concrete entities existing in a modal dimension. The mathematical and logical tools provided here allow for comprehensive analysis of modal reality.

Key Concepts:

  • Extended Modal Realism: Possible worlds exist as concrete entities, not just abstract constructs
  • Mathematical Correspondence: Prime numbers represent fundamental possible worlds with inverse size relationships
  • Modal Dimension: A dimension analogous to space and time where possible worlds exist
  • World Operations: Mathematical operations represent different ways of combining worlds
  • Proposition Analysis: Classical logic translation with modal and temporal indexing
